Andrew Garfield reveals the VERY surprising reason he lost out on role to Westworld's Ben Barnes

 Posted on March 14, 2024  |  5 minutes  |  861 words  |  Jenniffer Sheldon

Andrew Garfield has revealed he was almost cast as Prince Caspian in 2008’s The Chronicles of Narnia: Prince Caspian, however lost out on the role to Westworld star Ben Barnes because he wasn’t considered ‘handsome enough’.

Oscar-nominated Garfield, 38, explained that he was ‘desperate’ for the role and after hearing it had gone to Barnes, 40, he ‘incessantly nagged’ his agent in search of answers.

Speaking in a new interview, Garfield shared: ‘I auditioned for Prince Caspian and I thought, ”This could be it, this could be it.” And that handsome, brilliant actor Ben Barnes ended up getting the role.’

Oh dear: Andrew Garfield revealed he was almost cast as Prince Caspian in 2008’s The Chronicles of Narnia but lost out on the role because he wasn’t ‘handsome enough’

Speaking to ET, he added: ‘I think it was down to me and him, and I remember I was obsessed. She [Garfield’s agent] eventually broke under my nagging and was like ”It’s because they don’t think you’re handsome enough, Andrew.”’

However the star added that he’s ‘not unhappy with the decision in retrospect’ and sweetly noted that Barnes ‘did a beautiful job’.

The Chronicles of Narnia: Prince Caspian followed on from 2005’s Narnia film and was based on C. S. Lewis’ famous fantasy novel about a magical land hidden inside a wardrobe.

Nowadays, Barnes, who was 26 when he filmed The Chronicles of Narnia: Prince Caspian, is best known for starring as Logan Delos in Westworld and The Darkling in Netflix series Shadow and Bone.

Handsome man: Garfield lost out on the role to actor Ben Barnes who he said did a ‘beautiful’ job in the role (pictured in 2020)

Pictured: Barnes in character as Prince Caspian in a still from 2008’s Chronicles of Narnia: Prince Caspian

Garfield’s candid confession comes two days after he scooped a Golden Globe for Best Performance by an Actor in a Motion Picture, Comedy or Musical in Tick, Tick… Boom!.

Elsewhere, the award-winning star recently revealed that he and fellow Spider-Man actor Tobey Maguire snuck into an opening night screening of No Way Home, the latest instalment in the web-slinging franchise starring Tom Holland.

The actor detailed how the fellow Peter Parker’s watched their latest multiverse flick together in a packed theatre, and managed to remain completely undetected.

‘It was just a really beautiful thing to share together,’ he fondly recalled of the outing in conversation with ET while expressing his disbelief at pulling it off without a fan frenzy.

Best known: Nowadays, Barnes, who was 26 when he filmed The Chronicles of Narnia: Prince Caspian, is best known for starring as Logan Delos in Westworld (pictured)

He added: ‘I still can’t believe it happened. I snuck into a cinema on opening night and just watched with my baseball cap on and my mask. In fact, I was also with Tobey, me and Tobey snuck into a cinema together and no one knew we were there,’ he explained. ‘It was just a really beautiful thing to share together.’

Garfield continued to speak about his close bond with both Tobey and Tom Holland and how their legendary on-screen multiverse moment came to be — which he confessed continues to blow his mind.

‘First and foremost, I am a fan, so when Amy Pascal and Kevin Feige and John Watts…pitched it, it was like I was the fans in the audience, watching it onscreen for the first time with like, the three Spider-Men across universes sharing a frame together.

Spidey-skills! Garfield recently divulged that he and fellow Spider-Man actor Tobey Maguire, 46, actually snuck into see a public screening of No Way Home together while remaining completely undetected

Mind blowing multiverse moment: ‘And like, your head, your little head, it just explodes. So, that’s the main thing in me that I feel about it,’ Garfield shared of that memorable on-screen moment with Maguire and Holland in the latest Marvel instalment

‘And like, your head, your little head, it just explodes. So, that’s the main thing in me that I feel about it,’ The Amazing Spider-Man star shared.

‘The fact that I get to be one of those people wearing the suit next to my actual Spider-Man hero, Tobey Maguire, and the brilliant, incredibly talented, heartfelt, funny, good, sweet, perfect Spider-Man of Tom Holland…there’s something spiritual that kind of happened and happens,’ he went on to say.

The British-American actor continued to share that regardless of whether or not he gets the chance to slip on the Spider-Man suit again, it will always be part of his being.

‘It’s always going to be a part of me, whether I’m playing the character or not, I feel very connected to him.’

Garfield played the titular character in 2012’s The Amazing Spider-Man and 2014’s The Amazing Spider-Man 2, whereas Tobey first carried the title in the first three instalments of the Spider-Man film.
