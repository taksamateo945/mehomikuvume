Remembering Ahmad Givens, Star Of VH1s Real Chance of Love | How Did Ahmad Givens Die?

 Posted on February 22, 2024  |  2 minutes  |  356 words  |  Aldo Pusey

The Stallionares’ brothers Ahmad “Real” and Kamal “Chance” Givens, who were formerly participants on I Love New York, star in the American reality television dating game program Real Chance of Love. When Mr. Boston from the first season of I Love New York was offered his own reality series in August 2007, the idea for the show was first floated. Boston declined, and the plans ultimately fell through, leaving Real and Chance to pick up the show.

However, Real Chance of Love on VH1 star Ahmad Givens passed away on 20 February 2015. He was just 33 years old. Some reports say, he died on February 21, 2015, aged 35. Ahmad is survived by his widow Raquel, son Mahdi, and other relatives.

Givens, also known as “Real,” passed away after a protracted fight with cancer, according to his brother Kamal “Chance” Givens. Despite receiving chemotherapy after receiving a diagnosis of stage-four colon cancer in 2013, Ahmad’s condition reportedly deteriorated in January.

Chance posted a picture of himself crying on Instagram along with the words “Bye to my favourite man in the entire world — be in peace.” “We will always adore you, bro. I’ll meet you soon, and we’ll dance once more in God’s kingdom for all eternity, my brother. Words can’t express how I hurt; please pray for me, world!

Ahmad, a rapper from the Stallionaires, first made an appearance on VH1’s I Love New York before collaborating with Chance to participate in the 2008–2009 spinoff Real Chance of Love. Later, they appeared in the reality show Real and Chance: The Legend Hunters on VH1.
